Atrophy

Atrophy (originally called Heresy) is a thrash metal band that formed in 1986 in Tucson, Arizona. They were part of the second wave of thrash metal that swept through the USA with the likes of Testament, Dark Angel, Vio-lence, Forbidden, Flotsam and Jetsam, etc. In 1987, Atrophy released two cassette demos (“Chemical Dependency” and “Advanced Promo”) which helped eventually get them signed by Roadrunner Records. In 1988, they released their debut album “Socialized Hate”. In 1990, they released their second and (to this date) final record “Violent by Nature” before the band broke up in 1993. In 2015, they reformed and are planning on releasing a new album in 2022.

Socialized Hate (1988)

“Socialized Hate” came out on November 7th, 1988 on Roadrunner Records. It is Atrophy’s debut album and came out after they released two well-received cassette demos in “Chemical Dependency” (1987) and “Advanced Promo” (1987).

The album artwork depicts a Jester pulling USA and USSR nuclear warheads out of the ground – likely alluding to the arms race / Mutually Assured Destruction (M.A.D.) during the Cold War given the album’s 1988 release date. The cover illustration was done by Brian Anderson.

  • Brian Zimmerman (Vocals)
  • Chris Lykins (Guitars)
  • Rick Skowron (Guitars)
  • James Gulotta (Bass)
  • Tim Kelly (Drums)

Violent By Nature (1990)

“Violent by Nature” came out March 26th, 1990 on Roadrunner Records. It is Atrophy’s second and final album as of today. After releasing and touring for this album, guitarist Chris Lykins would soon leave the band to pursue a medical school course – eventually leading to the dissolution of the band in 1993.

“Violent by Nature” was recorded in Los Angeles and was co-produced by producer & engineer Bill Metoyer. The album artwork features a child playing with a toy tank, holding a gun, next to a bomb, and with a buried body in front of him. The child used to model for this artwork was Richie Bujinowski (now known as Richie Cavalera). He would later become Max Cavalera’s (Sepultura) adopted stepson and singer for the band Incite.

  • Brian Zimmerman (Vocals)
  • Chris Lykins (Guitars)
  • Rick Skowron (Guitars)
  • James Gulotta (Bass)
  • Tim Kelly (Drums)

At the end of the song, this individual looks back on what they have done in their life and feels regret for it – taking advantage of others and destroying nature out of pure greed for profit. Atrophy points out that it’s not too late to change and right your wrongs/become a better person.
