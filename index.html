Artillery

Artillery is a thrash metal band from Taastrup, Denmark. The band was formed in June 1982 by guitarist Jørgen Sandau and drummer Carsten Nielsen while they were visiting Danish heavy metal band Mercyful Fate at their rehearsal room. Named after the track “Heavy Artillery” by Tank, the band recorded the demos We Are the Dead, Shellshock, and Deeds of Darkness in 1983-1984.

In 1985, vocalist Carsten Lohmann left and was replaced by Flemming Rönsdorf. Later the same year, Artillery recorded a fourth demo, Fear of Tomorrow, and released their first full-length album, also titled Fear of Tomorrow. In 1986, drummer Carsten Nielsen was asked by Quorthon of Bathory if he was interested in drumming for Bathory, but Nielsen turned down the offer. Artillery’s second studio album, Terror Squad, was released in 1987.

Atrophy

Atrophy (originally called Heresy) is a thrash metal band that formed in 1986 in Tucson, Arizona. They were part of the second wave of thrash metal that swept through the USA with the likes of Testament, Dark Angel, Vio-lence, Forbidden, Flotsam and Jetsam, etc. In 1987, Atrophy released two cassette demos (“Chemical Dependency” and “Advanced Promo”) which helped eventually get them signed by Roadrunner Records. In 1988, they released their debut album “Socialized Hate”. In 1990, they released their second and (to this date) final record “Violent by Nature” before the band broke up in 1993. In 2015, they reformed and are planning on releasing a new album in 2022.

Socialized Hate (1988)

“Socialized Hate” came out on November 7th, 1988 on Roadrunner Records. It is Atrophy’s debut album and came out after they released two well-received cassette demos in “Chemical Dependency” (1987) and “Advanced Promo” (1987).

The album artwork depicts a Jester pulling USA and USSR nuclear warheads out of the ground – likely alluding to the arms race / Mutually Assured Destruction (M.A.D.) during the Cold War given the album’s 1988 release date. The cover illustration was done by Brian Anderson.

Violent By Nature (1990)

“Violent by Nature” came out March 26th, 1990 on Roadrunner Records. It is Atrophy’s second and final album as of today. After releasing and touring for this album, guitarist Chris Lykins would soon leave the band to pursue a medical school course – eventually leading to the dissolution of the band in 1993.

“Violent by Nature” was recorded in Los Angeles and was co-produced by producer & engineer Bill Metoyer. The album artwork features a child playing with a toy tank, holding a gun, next to a bomb, and with a buried body in front of him. The child used to model for this artwork was Richie Bujinowski (now known as Richie Cavalera). He would later become Max Cavalera’s (Sepultura) adopted stepson and singer for the band Incite.

Intruder

Intruder (originally known as "Transgresser") is an American thrash metal band from Nashville, Tennessee. The group began as a four-piece cover band. They saw the addition of rhythm guitarist Greg Messick after signing with Metal Blade Records. Intruder released three full-length albums and one EP from 1987 to 1991 before splitting up in 1992.

A Higher Form of Killing (1989)

The title of this album was taken from a quote by Professor Fitz Haber, Pioneer of Gas Warfare when he received the Nobel Peace Prize for Chemistry in 1919.

“In no future war will the military be able to ignore the use of poison gas. It is a Higher Form of Killing.”

Intruder’s drummer, John Pieroni, touched on all of the songs in Lost and Found Record’s 2019 re-release vinyl liner notes. His statements are in quotes for each song.

This is a short 1-minute long collection of skits where it is supposed to mimic the news.

“As for the intro of the song (referring to The Martyr) ‘Time of Trouble’, we had a blast making that. Keith Coes, who had worked with the band for years, had a friend who could impersonate many famous news announcers. So I wrote the scripted news blurbs to do in different voices, and there you have it – world famous newscasters on an Intruder record. Now that we have had terrorist acts on our own soil, it now makes it even more topical…and I always expect a visit from Homeland Security!”

This song is about what goes on in the mind of a terrorist. It was inspired by the Achille Lauro hijacking.

“In October of 1985, the Palestine Liberation Front hijacked the Achille Lauro, an Italian cruise ship. The terrorists killed a disabled American tourist and threw his body overboard with his wheelchair. As with the London bombings, and others, the world watched in horror as tragedy unfolded. As I watched the events, I tried to get into the mind of a terrorist, tried to understand why they do the things they do I also thought about how we as a nation (at the time…) never had to experience horror and bloodshed as other nations have with terrorist attacks on their own soil. The song is a trip into the terrorist mind and the way he (or she) justifies the things they do.”

This song is about a person who plays God with gene manipulation.

“Another one of my ‘be careful of technology’ warning songs. I had done a lot of research on what was then the new field of DNA gene research. Manipulation of genes intrigued me on a medical and ethical basis. I could see the enormous impact it would have to heal but I also could see how in the wrong hands a person could play God. This of course is what happens in this song.

This song is a summarization of the 5 books in Phillip Jose Farmer’s "Riverworld” series.

“Along with horror, science fiction is one of my favorite forms of fiction. The song is based on Phillip Jose Farmer’s "Riverworld” series. In these books, every man, woman, and child who dwelt on Earth during the first several million years of human existence until about the year 1983 are all resurrected simultaneously on Riverworld by the mysterious aliens known as Ethicals for purposes only they understand. I have attempted to condense the 5 books in the series into 1 song. Everyone read the series and let me know if I succeeded!"

(I'm Not Your) Stepping Stone is a cover off of The Monkees' 2nd studio album "More of the Monkees" (1967).

“Intruder does the Monkees! The blame falls on my shoulders for this one, as I am the admitted Monkees freak. As a kid watching Mickey Dolenz play drums on the Monkees TV show inspired me to play the drums. A live favorite, this song is just damn fun to play as well as a chance to give Jim a breather in the middle of the set (as I’d take over singing duties on this song). Back in the early days, we would play the theme song from the Monkees as a break from the seriousness of the songs. We used to also jam on ‘Stepping Stone’ at rehearsal. Lots of artists have covered this song including Jimi Hendrix, and the Sex Pistols to name a few. But at the time, no metal band had done it, so we went for it! We rearranged it, adding new lyrics in the bridge, and Arthur laid down the guitar solo from hell. Good fun. And the little drum thing at the end was my homage to Mickey Dolenz’s song ‘Randy Scouse Git’ where he does the exact same thing.”

This song is about poison gas and its use/experimentation around the time of WW1. Chemical weapons were a major part of WW1 and this was the first time that chemical weapons were widely used in warfare.

“This is one of the heaviest songs we play and one that is totally misunderstood by the Anti-Heavy Metal brigade. They claimed we were advocating ‘pro-killing’ (whatever the hell that is), but is really just the opposite. The title was taken from a quote by Professor Fitz Haber, Pioneer of Gas Warefare when he received the Nobel Peace Prize for Chemistry in 1919. ‘In no future war will the military be able to ignore the use of poison gas. It is a Higher Form of Killing.’ The song is about Portion Down, a research facility in the UK, where animals were bred and tortured under horrific conditions, and humans too, including servicemen, the elderly, and the mentally handicapped were being subjected to terrifying and dangerous experiments – all in the name of perfecting A Higher Form of Killing."

This song is an ode to Charles Bronson - an actor in the Death Wish films.

This song is about Men in Black (M.I.B.) - the mysterious beings that allegedly appear wherever a major UFO sighting is.

Here’s a brief summary about them: The Men in Black are beings who appear anywhere major UFO sightings take place. They go about threatening people who have claimed to have seen UFOs into not asking about what they witnessed. They wear sunglasses. They wear black suits. They have olive complexions. They drive mint-condition vintage luxury cars or fly in black helicopters. Some think they are government agents. I like to think Chris Carter of the X-files fame was inspired by thrashing to this tune…could have happened!”
